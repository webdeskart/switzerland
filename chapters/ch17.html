Chapter 17

Frequently Asked Questions

Visa and Documentation

Q: Do Indian citizens need a visa for Switzerland?
A: Yes, Indian citizens need a Schengen visa to visit Switzerland. Apply at the Swiss embassy or VFS Global center in India.
Q: How long does it take to get a Swiss visa?
A: Standard processing takes 15 calendar days. Apply 4-6 weeks before your trip.
Q: What is the Schengen visa fee for Indians?
A: The standard fee is EUR 90 for adults, EUR 45 for children aged 6-12, and free for children under 6. Additional VFS service fee applies.
Q: Can I apply for a Swiss visa online?
A: You fill out the application form online, but submission requires a physical appointment at the VFS center for biometrics.

Flights and Travel

Q: Which Indian cities have direct flights to Switzerland?
A: Delhi and Mumbai have direct flights to Zurich operated by Swiss International Air Lines.
Q: How long is the flight from India to Switzerland?
A: Direct flights take 8-9 hours. Connecting flights take 11-16 hours depending on the layover.
Q: What is the best time to book flights?
A: Book 3-4 months in advance for the best prices. Mid-week flights are usually cheaper.
Q: Which airlines are best for Indian travelers?
A: Swiss Air, Emirates, Qatar Airways, and Lufthansa are popular choices with good service and Indian meal options.

Money and Budget

Q: Is Switzerland expensive for Indian travelers?
A: Switzerland is expensive compared to India. A budget traveler needs CHF 100-150 per day, mid-range CHF 250-350 per day.
Q: Can I use Indian rupees in Switzerland?
A: No, Indian rupees are not accepted. Use Swiss Francs (CHF) or Euros (accepted at some places at lower rates).
Q: Are credit cards widely accepted in Switzerland?
A: Yes, Visa and Mastercard are accepted almost everywhere. American Express is less common.
Q: Should I carry cash in Switzerland?
A: Yes, carry some cash (CHF 200-300) for small purchases, public toilets, and markets. Most places accept cards.
Q: Is it cheaper to use ATMs or exchange money in India?
A: ATMs in Switzerland offer competitive exchange rates. However, check your bank's international transaction fees. Exchanging in India may also be convenient.

Food and Dining

Q: Is Indian food available in Switzerland?
A: Yes, Indian restaurants are available in all major cities. Zurich, Lucerne, Interlaken, and Geneva have excellent Indian dining options.
Q: Are there vegetarian food options in Switzerland?
A: Yes, Switzerland is very vegetarian-friendly. Most restaurants have vegetarian options, and Indian restaurants offer extensive vegetarian menus.
Q: Is Jain food available in Switzerland?
A: Jain food is available at select restaurants like Govinda's in Lucerne and Hiltl in Zurich. Call ahead to confirm Jain options.
Q: Can I find halal food in Switzerland?
A: Yes, many Indian and Middle Eastern restaurants offer halal food. Indian restaurants like Taj Mahal in Interlaken serve halal options.
Q: Is tap water safe to drink in Switzerland?
A: Yes, tap water in Switzerland is of excellent quality and safe to drink everywhere.

Transportation

Q: Is the Swiss Travel Pass worth it?
A: Yes if you are traveling between cities and plan to use mountain railways and visit museums. It offers excellent value for active travelers.
Q: How punctual are Swiss trains?
A: Swiss trains are famously punctual - over 90% arrive within 3 minutes of scheduled time.
Q: Can I use Uber in Switzerland?
A: Uber is available in Zurich, Geneva, Basel, and Lausanne. However, public transport is more reliable and cost-effective.
Q: Is it better to rent a car in Switzerland?
A: Not recommended unless exploring remote villages. Swiss public transport is excellent and renting a car is expensive with parking challenges.
Q: How do I buy train tickets?
A: Through the SBB Mobile app, ticket machines at stations, or online at sbb.ch. Buying in advance is recommended.

Accommodation

Q: What types of accommodation are available in Switzerland?
A: Options range from hostels (CHF 30-50) and budget hotels (CHF 80-120) to luxury 5-star hotels (CHF 300+). Airbnb and holiday apartments are also available.
Q: Should I book hotels in advance?
A: Yes, especially during peak season (June-August and December). Book at least 2-3 months in advance for best rates.
Q: Are there Indian-friendly hotels in Switzerland?
A: Many international hotel chains and Indian restaurants nearby areas are Indian-friendly. Hotels in Interlaken, Zurich, and Lucerne are accustomed to Indian guests.

Weather and Clothing

Q: What should I pack for Switzerland in summer?
A: Light cotton clothes, a warm jacket for evenings and mountains, comfortable walking shoes, rain jacket, sunscreen, and sunglasses.
Q: What should I pack for winter?
A: Thermal innerwear, warm sweaters, insulated waterproof jacket, hat, gloves, scarf, thermal socks, and waterproof boots.
Q: Does it rain a lot in Switzerland?
A: Rainfall is moderate and spread throughout the year. Always carry a light rain jacket or umbrella.

Health and Safety

Q: Do I need travel insurance for Switzerland?
A: Yes, travel medical insurance with minimum EUR 30,000 coverage is mandatory for a Schengen visa.
Q: Is Switzerland safe for solo female travelers?
A: Yes, Switzerland is very safe for solo female travelers. Standard precautions apply as with any destination.
Q: Are there any required vaccinations for Switzerland?
A: No specific vaccinations are required for Switzerland from India. Routine vaccinations should be up to date.
Q: What should I do in case of a medical emergency?
A: Dial 112 for European emergency services. Switzerland has excellent healthcare facilities. Your travel insurance will cover medical costs.

Shopping

Q: What should I buy in Switzerland?
A: Swiss watches, chocolates, Swiss Army knives, cheese, fondue sets, music boxes, and Matterhorn souvenirs are popular purchases.
Q: How does the tax refund work in Switzerland?
A: Spend CHF 300+ in a single store, get a Tax Free Form, have it stamped at customs when leaving, and collect your refund at the airport.
Q: Are shops open on Sundays in Switzerland?
A: Most shops are closed on Sundays, except at airports, train stations, and tourist areas.

Culture and Etiquette

Q: Is English widely spoken in Switzerland?
A: Yes, English is widely spoken in tourist areas, hotels, restaurants, and by younger Swiss people. Learn basic greetings in German, French, or Italian.
Q: What is the tipping culture in Switzerland?
A: Tipping is not mandatory as service is included. However, rounding up the bill or leaving 5-10% for good service is appreciated.
Q: Is it acceptable to wear Indian clothes in Switzerland?
A: Yes, wearing Indian clothes is perfectly acceptable. Switzerland is a multicultural country that respects diversity.

Connectivity

Q: Can I get an Indian SIM card to work in Switzerland?
A: Yes, with international roaming activated. However, local SIM cards or eSIMs offer better rates for data.
Q: Is free Wi-Fi available in Switzerland?
A: Yes, most hotels, cafes, and public spaces offer free Wi-Fi. SBB trains also have free Wi-Fi.
Q: Which is the best mobile network in Switzerland?
A: Swisscom has the best coverage, followed by Sunrise and Salt. All three offer reliable service in tourist areas.
