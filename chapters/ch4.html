Chapter 4

Budget Planning

Overview

Switzerland is known for being an expensive destination, but with careful planning, Indian travelers can enjoy a memorable trip without breaking the bank. This chapter provides detailed budget estimates for different travel styles and trip durations.

Budget Traveler (Backpacker)

Daily Budget: CHF 100-150 (₹9,500 - ₹14,250 per day)

Accommodation: CHF 30-50 (Hostels/Budget Hotels)
Food: CHF 25-40 (Supermarket meals, self-catering)
Transport: CHF 20-30 (Swiss Travel Pass, local buses)
Activities: CHF 15-25 (Free walking tours, parks, museums)
Miscellaneous: CHF 10-15 (Souvenirs, tips, incidentals)

Mid-Range Traveler

Daily Budget: CHF 250-350 (₹23,750 - ₹33,250 per day)

Accommodation: CHF 80-120 (3-star hotels, guesthouses)
Food: CHF 60-80 (Mid-range restaurants, Indian restaurants)
Transport: CHF 40-60 (Swiss Travel Pass, scenic trains)
Activities: CHF 40-60 (Mountain excursions, museums, boat trips)
Miscellaneous: CHF 30-40 (Shopping, tips, incidentals)

Luxury Traveler

Daily Budget: CHF 500-800 (₹47,500 - ₹76,000 per day)

Accommodation: CHF 200-350 (5-star hotels, luxury resorts)
Food: CHF 120-180 (Fine dining, Michelin-starred restaurants)
Transport: CHF 80-120 (First-class trains, private transfers)
Activities: CHF 80-120 (Helicopter tours, private guides, VIP experiences)
Miscellaneous: CHF 50-80 (Luxury shopping, premium experiences)

Money-Saving Tips for Indian Travelers
  • Book Swiss Travel Pass in advance to save on transportation.
  • Cook your own meals if staying in hostels or apartments with kitchen.
  • Eat at Indian restaurants or grocery stores like Migros and Coop for affordable food.
  • Visit free attractions like Old Towns, parks, and lakeside promenades.
  • Travel during shoulder season (April-May, September-October) for lower prices.
  • Use half-price cards for mountain excursions.
